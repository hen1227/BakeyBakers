BakeyBakers life

I first started when I wanted a bake sale, but I thought our fold-out tables were too dirty. My mom offered if I gave her the next two hundred dollars I make from the Bake Sale She would make me a cart that I could use. On my first Bake Sale, I thought it was fun, so jokingly started calling it "Bakey Bakers.". I now own a website called BakeyBakers.com.




I am sorry for the pain in reading that sentance I wrote in 6th grade... writing isn't really my strong suit. A more gramaticly correct story (9th grade):




In fifth grade, I was intrigued by the idea of having a business and being able to put my profits back into the business to make it better. I had one successful bake sale and when I packed up my stuff and asked my mom to get a better stand. I gave her the money I had earned and she built me a beautiful stand. I was at about $180 in debt. I doubt she was expecting me to pay it back but I did. I spent a couple of weekends, occasionally with a friend, and got the money to her. I was so happy and proud of myself. I had made no money but I was out of debt. It took me all of a week before investing in a tiny gumball machine.


I held another couple of stands and made a small profit but it was around this time I got a laptop. I had been experimenting in making websites but nothing more than just hello world and buttons that showed a joke. I talked to a couple of friends who had an actual website and I started my Bakeybakers website. I didn't really know what to have on it and for about a month it was just the "BakeyBakers Life" section above. It was this time when I took a coding class on an app called processing. I loved it! In between the weekly classes I was really simple things like a ball that bounced around the screen. I quickly got ahead of the class and started making my own games and spent the class coding my games. The class did teach me things I couldn't find wiki pages on. I felt that I was actually creating something. If only there was a way to put these games on my website. That is when I learned about The Coding Train.
